**Brief History** - Carrabelle, a hidden gem nestled along Florida's Panhandle, is a testament to resilience and charm. With a history marked by the record fourth landfall of Tropical Storm Fay in 2008, this coastal town boasts a rich maritime heritage. It's a place where the Carrabelle River meets the Gulf of Mexico, enveloped by the beauty of Dog Island and Tate's Hell State Forest. The climate is warm, allowing for year-round outdoor activities, making it a sanctuary for those who seek solace in nature's embrace【6†source】.

**Crooked River Lighthouse**: Marvel at this historic beacon, guiding mariners since the 19th century. Climb to the top for panoramic views of the Gulf and a sunset that paints the sky in hues of gold and pink.

**World’s Smallest Police Station**: Snap a photo at this quirky landmark, a phone booth turned police station, encapsulating Carrabelle's charm and wit.

**Tate's Hell State Forest**: Dive into adventure across 202,000 acres of untamed wilderness. Hike, camp, or trail-ride through ecosystems teeming with wildlife, from eagles to black bears.
